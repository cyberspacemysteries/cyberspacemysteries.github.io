how 2 guide

to nip05 website verification

on your GitHub website (without using a terminal)

Tie your nostr identity to your website

Logged into github.com, find your website's repository.

Hint | it ends with .github.io

Click on the button Add file, then click Create new file

Type:

.well-known/

Hint | well-known must have a period . before & a forward slash / after

Type:

nostr.json

Select, copy and paste the following code to the body of the file:


          {
            "names": {
              "mynostrhandle": "myhexkey"
            }
          }


Between the second set of quotation marks replace mynostrhandle with your nostr handle, without the @ symbol.

In nostr you can choose any handle you want. If you change it, remember to come back and update this file.

Between the third set of quotation marks replace myhexkey with your hex key.

If you do not know your hex key, no worries, keep scrolling down.

Remember to click on Save, or in this case Commit changes

(?_?) My hex key?

Hopefully you know your npub or public key by now

If you don't, please refer to step 2 in Nostering in 5 steps

Enter https://damus.io/key

In the first box, paste your public key (starts with npub)

This Damus page will convert you npub key to a hex key in an instant

Copy your hex key and paste it in you nostr.json file (as mentioned earlier)

Let nostr products read your nostr.json

Back in your repository, let's create another file

Click on the button Add file, then click Create new file

Type:

_config.yml

In the body of the file, type:

include: [".well-known"]

*source

Remember to save

Add your nip05 to your nostr profile

It's your handle and your website's domain:

mynostrhandle@mywebsite.com

The steps to add it to your profile may vary based on which nostr product (client) you're using.

Congrats! You've tied your nostr to your website
