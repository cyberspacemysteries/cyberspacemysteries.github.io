nostr glossary




  • client

    Nostr is NOT just one app.

    A nostr client is an app or website created with nostr code. Since it is open code, any developer can build their own version of a nostr client.

    You may think of Nostr as social media, but it is a growing universe of clients going beyond what we are used to. We're starting to see blogging, marketplaces, music...

  • keys

    Not your keys, not your social media.

    You don't need to give up your email or phone number to join nostr. Instead, the first time you sign in to a nostr client, you get a special set of keys (long strings of numbers unique to you) that you can use to enter most of the nostr universe. They belong only to you. You get a public key and a secret key.

  • note

    Instead of tweets or posts, nostr has notes. Feel free to write notes away.

  • npub or public key

    npub + LongStringOfNumbersUniqueToYou

    Your public key takes the place of your handle. It is a number unique to you. So, you can pick whichever name you want as your handle, without the need for added numbers or strange spellings.

    Share your public key with your friends, so they can find you. You can copy it from your profile (often found by a key icon ).

    If you spot a QR icon in your nostr client, others who have a nostr app installed can scan it and their app should open to your profile page, as long as their app has that functionality. Some nostr clients let you share other people's npub QR's too.

  • nsec a.k.a. secret or private key

    You are the sole keeper of your secret key:

    Your nsec is a special kind of password. You can use it to sign in to nostr clients, and to create notes.

    nsec + LongStringOfNumbersUniqueToYou

    It belongs only to you, not to a company. If you are used to the ways of platforms like Twitter and Facebook, you may think someone can help you recover it, but you won't find a "forgot your password?" option.

    Nostr is different, you're the one in control. When someone owns your account, they know your password and can help you recover it. One of the tradeoffs is that they can also close down your account or limit it in some way. At nostr you own your account and should keep your nsec safe.

    TIP: Save your nsec in a password manager (for example Bitwarden).
    Some nostr clients keep your secret key hidden in the Settings. Make sure you copy it and save it in your password manager before logging out.
    In the future, we may see options to save an encrypted copy in the cloud, perhaps some options already exist.

    If you are logging in through a website (not an app), save your nostr nsec in a special browser extension first. Browsers are not as safe, your secret key could get stolen. You may see this option to log in labelled as "login with extension or NIP-07"

  • protocol

    Nostr is a protocol, not a platform

    Nostr is a protocol, which means its code is open so any developer can create unique apps and websites. You can log in to most of them with one account. Think of it like being able to enter either Facebook or Twitter, and have your identity in both of them, regardless of which one you use. Anyone could find you on either of them, and you could login to both of them or just one of them and find your same notes, follows, followers... You could send your notes on either of them and see them in both. The magic of nostr. Discover the nostrverse.

  • relays

    Relays help make nostr decentralized & censorship resistant

    Notes are spread out unto cyberspace through servers running nostr software, called relays. Relays make nostr special, because they do not rely on a single company, and they do not own your account. Anyone who's a little tech savvy could run a relay.

    The nostr relays keep your information and share it with other relays. Adding a few relays to your nostr account is a way to make sure your information is still available even if a relay were to go down. It is also a way to be able to read the posts of the people you follow. Some nostr clients add a set of relays automatically, others suggest them based on your follows.

    If you add too many relays, your nostr experience could slow down. What's the magic number 8!?.

    Some relays are paid and they may have less spam.

    Comprehensive relay list: nostr.watch

    TIP: Use a VPN service to protect your IP (like Mullvad). Nostr does not gather and sell your information like other platforms, but relays can know your IP.
